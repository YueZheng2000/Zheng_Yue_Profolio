This was me, 2006

When a Young Man Started His Educational Journey

Born in 2000 in a coastal city called Xiamen, China. I spent the first six years of my life in a coastal village, surrounded by a large, loving family. Then, I moved to the city, where I spent the next 12 years as a "good student," navigating the challenges and opportunities of urban life.

It was me, 2018

Commencing Undergrad in the Field of Business

After 12 years of hard work, I was admitted to one of China's top finance and economics universities, Southwestern University of Finance and Economics, with a score ranked in the top 2% in the National College Entrance Examination. Driven by my passion for mathematics and my strong foundation in it during my primary and secondary education, I chose to major in Economic Statistics.

As someone who was eager to apply what I had learned, I began conducting research for my course papers in Chinese, which, looking back now, seems quite amusing and immature both in terms of research methods and objectives. Some examples include a Survey on Students' Preferences for Food Delivery Services and a Study on the Sewage Treatment Model in a Village Called Anlong (The field research was planned but canceled due to the pandemic). At the same time, recognizing the importance of practical experience, I also participated in an internship at the Agricultural Bank of China during my first summer break at university, where I gained solid communication skills by solely dealing with some of the clients in the Premium Account sector.

Sorry that the papers linked here are in Chinese, as my undergraduate studies were conducted in Chinese. (>_<)

It was since 2020

My First Encounter with Machine Learning

When the pandemic disrupted all in-person classes and practical activities, I had my first formal encounter with machine learning, a subject that would later have a profound impact on me.

Teaming up with some senior students who were well-versed in machine learning in a statistical modeling competition on precise identification of high-risk customers, organized by Xinwang Bank, I came to realize the necessity of machine learning for statistics in the new era.

Since then, I gradually transitioned from using more straightforward, GUI-based statistical software like EViews to Stata—a more advanced statistical software that includes certain machine learning methods as well as complex statistical analysis techniques. Mastering these statistical software tools has also made empirical research much more manageable for me, which contributed to my high scores in my bachelor's thesis about the factors influencing industrial agglomeration in major Chinese cities.

On my way to ANU, 2022

Charting My Path Toward Computing

After earning a bachelor's degree in Economic Statistics, my passion for Machine Learning led me to pursue a master's degree with a strong focus on coding. I chose the two-year Computing program at the Australian National University (ANU) for the reason that it can offer the solid foundation I need to overcome my limited coding experience and develop custom ML models for the research I am passionate about.

During my studies at ANU, I gradually developed the ability to implement basic PC board games, Android applications, and conduct simple Machine Learning research in areas like bio-inspired computing and network science. Additionally, courses in data science have enhanced my critical thinking about the impact of AI and reinforced my understanding of the valuable insights we can extract from data.

It was from 2022 as well

Enhancing My Professional Expertise Through Practical Experience

As I gradually improved my coding and professional skills, I had the fortunate opportunity to join the Chinese National Social Science Fund Major Project, 'Research on the Path Selection for the Implementation of Mixed Ownership Reform in State-Owned Enterprises,' at Chinese Academy of Social Sciences as a student intern. This eventually led me to produce a related paper on the Ethical Regulation of Digital Technology in Australia.

After acquiring a certain level of expertise, I participated in a project under the ANU TechLauncher program as part of my course requirements. Over the course of a year, I worked with two companies, Mekai and AIDX, where I focused on refining front-end frameworks and implementing deepfake detection algorithms, respectively. These experiences not only allowed me to apply my technical knowledge in real-world settings but also significantly enhanced my professional skills and deepened my ability to collaborate effectively within diverse teams.

This is how I became the person I am today.
